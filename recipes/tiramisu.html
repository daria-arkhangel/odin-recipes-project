Tiramisu

Description:

Tiramisu, meaning “pick-me-up” in Italian, is a classic Italian dessert known for its layers of coffee-soaked ladyfingers, creamy mascarpone filling, and a dusting of cocoa powder. This no-bake treat is both light and indulgent, with hints of coffee and cocoa complemented by the rich, silky mascarpone. It’s the perfect way to end an Italian meal.

Ingridients:

  • 6 egg yolks
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup mascarpone cheese
  • 1 ½ cups heavy cream
  • 1 ½ cups strong brewed espresso, cooled
  • 2 tbsp coffee liqueur (optional)
  • 1 pack of ladyfinger cookies
  • Unsweetened cocoa powder for dusting
  • Dark chocolate shavings for garnish (optional)

Steps:

  1. Make Mascarpone Cream: In a bowl, whisk egg yolks and sugar until thick and pale. Gently fold in mascarpone until smooth.
  2. Whip Cream: In another bowl, whip heavy cream until stiff peaks form. Gently fold whipped cream into the mascarpone mixture.
  3. Prepare Coffee Soak: In a shallow dish, mix espresso and coffee liqueur if using. Dip each ladyfinger briefly into the coffee mixture, careful not to over-soak.
  4. Layer Tiramisu In an 8x8-inch dish, arrange a layer of coffee-dipped ladyfingers. Spread half of the mascarpone mixture over the ladyfingers. Repeat layers with remaining ladyfingers and mascarpone
  5. Chill: Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or overnight for best flavor.
  6. Serve: Dust with cocoa powder before serving. Top with chocolate shavings if desired.
