Carbonara

Description:

A classic Italian pasta dish, carbonara is loved for its creamy, rich sauce made with eggs, cheese, pancetta, and black pepper. Simple yet incredibly flavorful, this Roman specialty is a quick and satisfying meal. The secret to a great carbonara lies in using high-quality ingredients and timing â€“ the sauce should coat the pasta without turning into scrambled eggs.

Ingridients:

  • 12 oz spaghetti
  • 4 oz pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt for pasta water

Steps:

  1. Cook Pasta: Boil a pot of salted water and cook spaghetti until al dente. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water before draining.
  2. Cook Pancetta: In a skillet over medium heat, cook pancetta until crispy. Remove from heat.
  3. Prepare Sauce: In a bowl, whisk together eggs, cheese, and a generous amount of black pepper.
  4. Combine Pasta and Sauce: Add the hot, drained pasta to the skillet with pancetta, tossing to coat. Slowly pour in the egg mixture, tossing quickly to create a creamy sauce. Add reserved pasta water as needed to reach desired consistency.
  5. Serve: Serve immediately, topped with more Pecorino and black pepper.
